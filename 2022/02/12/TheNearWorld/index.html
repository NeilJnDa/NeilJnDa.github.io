The Near World
Feb 12, 2022

Worked as the programmer, narrative designer, and team leader

Click here to download

Inspiration

“Is it possible for the past self to save the future self?”

In a world where time travel does not exist, a self-saving story spanning seven years.

Nai lives alone in the Old City, unable to escape the pressure of her family and decides to give up everything. The unexpected appearance of the AI robot N-16 changed all. Its past is full of mysteries. What happened seven years ago? Does this AI have feelings?

Meet different people at the bar, try to form a band and fulfill a dream. In this bizarre city, this is your story.

 

Emotional Connection

In a game where the story experience is the core, the sense of immersion comes from emotional connection to a large extent.

  • The connection between the player and the protagonist Nai allows the player to feel Nai’s situation and feelings.
  • There is also a connection between the player (Nai) and the robot N-16. Players need to feel the changes brought by N-16, and treat N-16 as a person instead of a machine.

Scene Text

Before being accompanied by N-16, Nai’s emotional world was not harmonious. She is often trapped by words from mothers who don’t understand her. In order for players to understand Nai’s plight, these words are placed in the scene.

Scene text is not just decoration. When the player tries to go out, they will be blocked by the text at the door. Along with the visual effects and the sound effects of the heartbeat sound, the player’s motivation to continue the plot and hope to escape from the room will be basically the same as Nai’s motivation to try to escape from life’s difficulties. Like Nai, players are looking forward to some new changes.

Choice and Dialogue

Games are a series of meaningful choices. In The Near World, there are plenty of dialogue options.

In the bar, N-16 wants to know the taste of wine out of curiosity. The player needs to choose a wine and choose how to tell the AI the taste of the wine. “Taste for N-16” is an attempt to help AI experience the world. Players need to think about how each way of telling affects their relationship, and realize that the choice can help AI.

In addition, when telling the wind “New World”, the separation of society is also presented to a certain extent. The feeling of tasting “Mojito” tells about the misfortune of Nai’s childhood.

A better example is that the player first generates an idea spontaneously, and then finds the “voice” in the options.

After N-16 suddenly disappeared, Nai received a call from a friend at home and was asked how she is doing. The choices here allow players to determine their relationship with AI.

Is N-16 just a machine without emotion, or is it a good friend to accompany Nai?

Help in Mini Game

Persuade the former bassist to join the band, Nai needs to surpass him by 100 points in this pinball game.

This Mini game is like a tribute to the earliest game console Pong. Each player controls a baffle to hit the ball, and when the opponent does not hit the ball, he scores a point. For the player’s opponent to be more like a human (rather than simply moving with the position of the ball), I used the ML-Agents tool provided by Unity to apply deep reinforcement learning to achieve the AI of the opponent.

Interestingly, there is no way in reality to give AI real emotions like in games, but AI can perform very well when playing the mini game. The picture below shows the RL training process.

For players, this game is very easy to get started, but difficult to beat the AI opponent who has experienced tens of millions of training.

However, N-16 will resolve this difficulty for players. N-16 hopes to take the risk to invade the console and cheat to help players win. Players need to consider the risk and choose to take a chance or stop it. But in any case, N-16 will insist on expressing its gratitude to Nai by what it can do.

Although players and Nai have different sources of difficulty and different rewards, they all got help from N-16. Therefore, the players’ emotional identification with N-16 is completed. And the experience gained is the same as Nai’s, which is the gratitude for being helped.
